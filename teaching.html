Integrated Communication and Sensing Systems Group

Teaching

At the Integrated Communication and Sensing Systems Group, we are passionate about providing high-quality education to our students. The following courses are currently being taught by our team:

EE 400 – Digital and Analog Electronic Circuits

Operational Amplifiers and their applications; BJT differential and multistage amplifiers; IC Biasing; BJT current mirrors and current sources; MOS amplifiers; MOS current sources and current mirrors; Cascade amplifiers; Feedback amplifiers; Digital logic circuits; CMOS and pseudo NMOS logic circuits.

EE 407 – Electronic Communication Circuits

Radio frequency tuned amplifiers. Power amplifiers. Tuned LC oscillators. Crystal oscillators. Automatic gain control. Mixers. High frequency models of BJT. S-parameters. Introduction to Microwave devices: HBT and MESFET.

EE 512 – Applications of Integrated Circuits

Review of applications of op-amp.; Advanced applications of Op-Amp; Current feedback amplifiers and current conveyers: theory and applications; Analog signal processing using current mode circuits, voltage multipliers/mixers and dividers; Modulators, analog switches and multiplexers/ demultiplexers; Timers and programmable timers; PWM, V/F and F/V circuits; PLL circuits; Power management circuits; Power amplifier circuits; Mixed Signal ICs : Programmable amplifies , filters and oscillators; Programmable power supply circuits; voltage reference; AD/DA circuits.

EE 507 – VLSI Design

The course is to introduce the basic concepts of Low level and High-level designs; Design of Application specific Integrated Circuits; Design methods; CAD Tools; Case Study

EE 516 – Selected Topics in Electronics (Communication Circuits)

This course introduces the student to advanced topics in the electronics specialization area. Topics of current interest will be offered.
