Integrated Communication and Sensing Systems Group

Research

Phased Array Systems for Next-Gen SATCOM

Our research focuses on the development of advanced phased-array terminals for SATCOM applications. We investigate novel architectures and design techniques to enhance performance, reduce power consumption, and improve the overall efficiency of these systems. Our work spans from the individual component level to system-level optimization, enabling us to deliver state-of-the-art solutions for future SATCOM networks.

Secure IoT and RFID Hardware

Security and privacy are critical aspects of the rapidly growing Internet of Things (IoT) and Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) ecosystems. Our group is researching innovative hardware solutions to enhance the security and robustness of these systems. We are developing new cryptographic primitives, secure communication protocols, and tamper-resistant hardware designs to protect sensitive data and ensure the integrity of IoT and RFID devices.

Adaptive Low-Power Wireless Sensor Networks

Our project aims to create wireless sensor nodes with extremely low power consumption. We are specifically concentrating on developing ultra-low power radios for asymmetric wireless communications. These radios will enable this system to run on either harvested energy or extended battery life-time.
